### 1. The "Paper Trail" & Legal Compliance (DPA 2012)
Your current audit focuses on the **technical** isolation of data, but lacks the **legal** isolation required by the **Philippine Data Privacy Act (DPA) of 2012**.
**Missing Feature:** **Tenant-Specific Terms & Conditions (T&C).** Because these are separate legal entities (Cooperatives), a member must "Sign" a T&C specifically for *that* tenant during onboarding. 
* **Missing Feature:** **Data Privacy Consent Dashboard.** Members should see a clear "What this Co-op sees" vs. "What Agapay (Superadmin) sees" toggle to ensure compliance.

### 2. Financial Reconciliation (The "Master vs. Member" Ledger)
[cite_start]You mentioned ensuring the **Statement of Account (SOA)** derives from a `business_ledger`[cite: 77], which is excellent. However, there is no mention of **Reconciliation**.
* **The Problem:** What happens if a database update fails halfway through a payment?
* [cite_start]**Missing Feature:** **End-of-Day (EOD) Reconciliation.** An automated "Pulse Check" in **Agapay Tanaw** [cite: 29] that compares the sum of all Member Wallets against the Co-op’s declared Treasury. If they don't match, the Admin gets an "Imbalance Alert."

### 3. The "Kaagapay" (Guarantor) Exit Strategy
[cite_start]Your PRD handles what happens when things go *wrong* (Liability Trigger)[cite: 82], but not when things stay *right* for too long.

* **The "Grey Area":** If a Kaagapay wants to leave the Cooperative entirely but is still "on the hook" for a borrower's active loan, the system needs a protocol for "Transferring Liability" to a new guarantor.

[cite_start]Your "Forgot Password" [cite: 2] [cite_start]and "Calamity Alerts" [cite: 86] need an SMS fallback to be "Anti-GCash" in terms of accessibility.

Your professor’s instruction on **Locked Access** is a cornerstone of "Software as a Service" (SaaS) and fintech. It prevents "feature creep" for users and manages risk for the cooperative. 

To make this platform more than just a utility, we need to lean into **Behavioral Economics**. Here is how to implement the locked access and the "hooks" to keep the **Kaagapay** (members) engaged and disciplined.

### 1. Implementing "Locked Access" (Tier-Gated UI)

The goal is to show members what they *could* have, but prevent them from touching it until they prove their reliability.

* **For Members (The "Loan Ladder"):**
    * [cite_start]**Visual Gates:** In the **Agapay Pintig** app, display higher-tier loan products (e.g., the ₱50,000 "Suki" loan) but with a "lock" icon and a progress bar[cite: 74].
    * [cite_start]**Requirement Tooltips:** When a user clicks a locked loan, the system should say: *"Tapusin ang 2 pang loan cycles para ma-unlock ito"* (Finish 2 more loan cycles to unlock this)[cite: 70, 72].
    * [cite_start]**Math Transformation:** Only "Elite" users should even see the option for **Declining Balance** math, which is a reward for financial literacy[cite: 75].
* **For Lenders (Role-Based Permissions):**
    * [cite_start]**Scoped Authority:** Lenders can view member profiles and vouch scores but are "locked out" of changing branch-wide hex colors or global loan products, as those are Admin/Superadmin responsibilities[cite: 52, 13].
    * [cite_start]**Approval Siloing:** Lenders may have "Partial Approval" rights, where they verify the documents, but the "Final Release" button remains locked until an Admin clears it[cite: 30, 47].

### 2. Psychological Hooks: Making Agapay "Sticky"

To compete with the convenience of GCash, Agapay must use **Social Capital** and **Progressive Rewards**.

#### **A. The "Suki" Prestige System (Status Hook)**
In Philippine culture, being a *"Suki"* isn't just about being a customer; it’s a relationship. 
* **Feature:** Give Tier 3 users a digital "Suki Badge" in the app.
* **Psychology:** This acts as **Social Proof**. [cite_start]When they vouch for someone else, their badge appears next to their name, making their endorsement more valuable[cite: 9, 12].

#### **B. Interest Rate "Level-Downs" (Variable Reward)**
Instead of a flat rate, frame interest as something the user has the power to change.
* [cite_start]**Feature:** If a user pays 100% of their installments early for three months, the interest rate for their *next* loan drops by 0.5% (down to your 3% floor)[cite: 43].
* **The Formula:** $$Interest_{next} = Rate_{base} - (Reliability\_Score \times Multiplier)$$
* **Psychology:** This triggers **Loss Aversion**. Once they "earn" a 3.5% rate, they will work twice as hard to not "lose" it by being late.

#### **C. The "Vouch" Streak (Social Reciprocity)**
* **Feature:** Create a "Community Hero" score. [cite_start]Every time a person you vouched for pays on time, your own Trust Meter grows[cite: 12, 16].
* **Psychology:** This creates **Group Accountability**. It’s no longer just about their debt; it’s about their friend’s reputation.

#### **D. Milestone Celebrations (Dopamine Hits)**
* **Feature:** When a loan is fully paid (*Clearance*), don't just show a list. [cite_start]Show a "Certificate of Completion" or an animation of the **Asenso** sun rising[cite: 77]. 
* **Psychology:** This provides a sense of **Achievement** and closure, making the user eager to start the next cycle.
